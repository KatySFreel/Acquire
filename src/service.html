Frequently Asked Questions

01 How does affiliate marketing work for brands?

Affiliate marketing is a performance-based marketing channel where brands reward partners for driving measurable outcomes such as sales or leads. Affiliates promote products through content websites, media publishers, cashback platforms, loyalty programmes, and influencer channels using trackable links. When a customer completes a purchase through that link, the affiliate earns a commission. Because payment is tied to actual performance, affiliate marketing is considered one of the most cost-efficient digital marketing channels.

02 What does an affiliate marketing agency actually do?

An affiliate marketing agency manages the entire affiliate programme for a brand. This typically includes selecting the right affiliate network or tracking platform, recruiting partners, negotiating placements, setting commission structures, monitoring performance, and preventing fraud. Agencies also analyse partner performance, identify new growth opportunities, and ensure affiliate activity generates incremental revenue rather than simply rewarding existing traffic.

03 How long does it take for an affiliate programme to grow?

Affiliate programmes usually begin generating results within the first few months, but strong programmes typically take 6–12 months to mature. Early growth comes from recruiting key partners such as publishers and loyalty platforms, while long-term success depends on building relationships, expanding the partner mix, and continuously optimising placements and commission strategies.

04 What types of affiliate partners can promote a brand?

Affiliate partners can include content publishers, product review sites, media publishers, cashback platforms, loyalty programmes, price comparison websites, and influencer affiliates. Each partner type plays a different role within the customer journey. For example, editorial publishers often support discovery, while cashback and loyalty platforms help convert users closer to purchase.

05 Do I need an affiliate marketing agency or can I run a programme in-house?

Some brands manage affiliate programmes internally, but many choose to work with agencies due to the complexity of partner recruitment, performance optimisation, and tracking technology. Agencies typically bring established publisher relationships, negotiation expertise, and strategic insight that can accelerate programme growth and ensure the channel delivers incremental revenue.

06 How do brands track affiliate sales accurately?

Affiliate programmes rely on specialised tracking platforms provided by affiliate networks or tracking technologies. These systems use referral links, cookies, and server-side tracking to attribute conversions to specific partners. Advanced programmes may also use cross-device attribution and fraud prevention tools to ensure accurate measurement.

07 How much does affiliate programme management cost?

Affiliate marketing costs typically include affiliate commissions, platform or network fees, and agency management fees. Commission rates vary by industry but commonly range between 5% and 20% of the sale value. Agency fees may be structured as a monthly retainer, a percentage of programme revenue, or a hybrid model.

08 Is affiliate marketing suitable for all businesses?

Affiliate marketing works best for businesses with strong online conversion paths and competitive margins. E-commerce brands, subscription services, fintech platforms, travel companies, and digital services often see strong results from affiliate partnerships.
